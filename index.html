Not your old vending machine

Micro-market vs. vending machine.

A micro-market is an open, grab-and-go store — real shelves, glass-front coolers, fresh food, and tap-to-pay self-checkout. A vending machine is a locked box with a few dozen packaged snacks. Same break room, a completely different experience.

Micro-market — grab & go

  • Open shelves and coolers you walk right up to
  • 150–400+ items: fresh meals, drinks, snacks & coffee
  • Handle products, read labels, grab several at once
  • Fast tap-to-pay self-checkout
  • Shoppers spend more per visit

Traditional vending

  • A locked box — no touching before you buy
  • ~40 packaged, shelf-stable items
  • One item at a time; jams and dead rows
  • Coin or card slot
  • Limited selection, lower spend
